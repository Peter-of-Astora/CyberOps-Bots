1.Central server subnet hosting critical services for the entire organization. Includes web servers, database servers, and other infrastructure servers.
2.Research and Development department. This subnet generates internal R&D traffic and accesses internal servers. Hosts various client workstations used by researchers.
3.Management Department. Handles administrative functions and internal communications. Traffic may include access to financial records and management systems.
4.Technician Department. Responsible for technical support and infrastructure monitoring. May have higher network privileges and generate different traffic patterns.
5.Secretary and Operation Department. Handles day-to-day operational tasks, external communications, and customer-facing activities.
6.Building Management Subnet: Controls building utilities including HVAC, lighting, and access control systems.
7.Video Surveillance Subnet: Operates IP cameras and recording servers for physical security monitoring.
8.DMZ/External Services Subnet: Hosts internet-facing services like remote monitoring portals and VPN gateways.
9.Wireless Sensor Network: Comprises low-power non-IP devices using protocols like Zigbee or LoRaWAN for specialized sensing.
10.Development/Test Subnet: Isolated environment for testing new IoT devices and applications before production deployment.
